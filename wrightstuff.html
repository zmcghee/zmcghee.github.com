Perhaps, like me, you are incredibly fucking jealous of people who live in Los Angeles and have daily access to the glut of cinematic delights offered by the New Beverly Cinema. Upon contemplating the lineup for next week's "The Wright Stuff III: Movies Edgar Has Never Seen," the third film festival programmed (and hosted?) by Edgar Wright, super-awesome filmmaker extraordinare.

As much as I would like to skip out on work and splurge on a cross-country flight and eight nights of hotels, sanity prevails (at least this year). And while clearly nothing can compare with seeing these films projected in glorious 35mm, I nevertheless have been contemplating following along at home. So, just in case anyone in the U.S. is a big nerd like me*, I've taken the liberty of compiling your viewing options.

Most troubling are Get Crazy (1983), which has never been released on DVD and exists only on VHS; The Umbrellas of Cherbourg (1964), which is out of print on DVD; and To Be or Not to Be (1942), which is also out of print, but is available via Hulu and (allegedly) by mail from Blockbuster. If you still have a local video store, it's probably your best bet in tracking down those films in particular.

Finally, if you have a correction or would like to add to this information in any way, you can (1) send me an email or (2) fork this file on Github and send me a pull request with your changes.

*Although clearly no one is as big a nerd as me.
